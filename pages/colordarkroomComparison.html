Comparing color film and digital

Color film is a medium which is mostly in the history books. There are many reasons why the digital has supplanted color film as the medium people use for general photography. I thought it'd be interesting to compare digital and film prints to see how they stack up against each other.

The following photos were taken for my night photography project in our color darkroom class at NESOP. I first photographed them digitally to know the settings that I wanted for the film camera. Then I took the exact same photo with film. The film was probably Kodak Portra ISO 400 film. If I took a photo under strong artificial light I added a blue cooling filter to the film camera. This meant that instead of having a photo that looked super yellow, it'd just look normal. My camera was a Nikon FE.

One could argue that many of the film photos have more emotion. The inability to capture some of the darker areas adds emotion to the photos. One could also argue that the digital photos are simply much clearer and crisper. My hope is to put forth this comparison so that people can have a better idea for themselves as to the differences between the mediums.
