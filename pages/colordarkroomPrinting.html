Color darkroom printing in action

After shooting your film and getting back your negatives, you take your negatives and put them in sleeves. This lets you look at all your photos at once. You can also print this collection of color sleeves to get a better sense of how the photos look.

Next you choose a negative (or more) to print. I chose negative #31. Note that the scratches may not have existed when I printed this, or the scratches are on the plastic cover and not on the negative. Note that the red x was written on the plastic cover.

I kept color correcting each print until I got the best color balance in the photo. Note that adding to a color filter means that you're removing more of that color from the photo. When I filter out more yellow from the photo, that makes the photo less yellow. Because yellow and blue are complementary colors, this would make the photo look more blue. When I filter out less yellow from the photo, it makes the photo look more yellow. Definitely confusing; makes you happy that you send this to Kodak/CVS to figure it out.

Doing this with digital photos and bubblejets is much easier. A while ago that wasn't an option; this page is dedicated to knowing more about how this process used to be done.
